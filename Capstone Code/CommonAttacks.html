Common Attacks

There are many common malicious cyber attacks that occur.

A cyber attack occurs when a user tries to breach an information

system of an individual or business.

Types of Common Attacks in Cybersecurity

Malware

Malware encompasses several different types of attacks

including viruses, worms, and spyware. Malware utilizes a

vulnerability in order to breach a network when a user clicks

a suspicious link or email attachment, which is utilized to

install malicious software inside the system.

Ransomware

A ransomware attack happens when the targeted user may accidentality download ransomware,

this can come from either a website or an email link. However, this is very dangerous

and this can affect multiple computers. If this happens, the victim’s system is compromised and now will

be held hostage until they agree to repay the attacker.

Phishing

Phishing attacks are very common. Phishing is the act of sending

numerous amounts of fradulent emails to users, typically claiming

to come from a reliable source. These kind of attacks can occur through

emails, text messages, and social media.

It is important to recognize what a phishing email or text message looks like.

They may come from a company that is known and trusted. These emails/text messages

frequently will tell some kind of story as a ploy to click on a link or attachment.

Some strategies to protect yurself from phishing:

1. Frequently update your software so it can deal with new security threats

2. Make sure your cell phone's software updates automatically

3. Use multi-factor authentication for extra security when logging in to your account

4. Back up your data on your phone

Denial of Service (DOS)

DOS attacks operate by flooding systems or networks with traffic as a way

to overload resources. This, in turn, renders the system unable to process

and deliver authorized requests. However, since these are illegitimate requests of high quantity, the site

will be consumed and make it impossible for the site to function properly.

Password Attack

Passwords are the most commonly used way of user authentication to a secure

information system. This makes them a vulnerable target to malicious cyber

attackers. As soon as an attacker gains the password to another person's cell

phone, laptop, or any other device, they gain access to the user's information,

especially personal information.

They can be done in a few different ways.

However, this could be if the attacker finds the password or pays someone to

get the password that is written down in the office, for example.

A hacker may also use a dictionary attack that will use common words to try and guess

the target’s passwords. The hacker may also try to intercept network transmission

to grab passwords that are not encrypted by the network.
